Sequoia National Park

Location

Sequoia National Park is adjacent to Kings Canyon National Park in California's southern Sierra Nevada mountains. Check out the map Below!

Hiking

There are more than 1,000 miles of Sequoia & Kings Canyon hiking trails! Top-rated Sequoia hikes include Big Trees Trail, Crescent Meadow Loop Trail, Congress Trail, Tokopah Falls Trail, Hazelwood Nature Trail, Alta Peak Trail, and the High Sierra Trail to Bearpaw Meadow.

Where to stay

Cabins and camp sites! Make sure to reserve your spot!

Reservations

Points of Interest

Sequoia is known for its huge sequoia trees, notably the General Sherman Tree dominating the Giant Forest. The underground Crystal Cave features streams and striking rock formations.

Yosemite National Park

Location

Yosemite is located in eastern central California in the United States. West of the park are the major cities of San Francisco and Sacramento, California.

Tours

Yosemite Hospitality offers bus tours year-round. All tours begin in Yosemite Valley. Make sure to check out the different tours that are offered!

Tours

Where to stay

Yosemite National Park has 13 popular campgrounds, of which up to seven are on a reservation system. From April through September, reservations are essential and even the first-come, first-served campgrounds usually fill by noon (often earlier) from April through September.

Reservations

Fishing

Yosemite is home to a number of fish, including crappie, brown, golden, brookies and rainbow trout, and trout stream fishing is probably your best bet in the summer. The park has 58 permanent streams with a combined length of 770 miles. The Merced offers enjoyable fishing all summer and into the winter.

More

Activities at Yosemite are endless. From Rock climbing to horseback riding to swimming and birdwatching. There's plenty of fun to be had at Yosemite.

Yellowstone National Park

Location

Yellowstone National Park is a nearly 3,500-sq.-mile wilderness recreation area atop a volcanic hot spot. Mostly in Wyoming, the park spreads into parts of Montana and Idaho too.

Thermal Features

Yellowstone National Park preserves an incredible array of hot springs, mudpots, fumaroles and is home to more than half of the world's geysers.

Where to stay

Lodging and camping! Make sure to reserve your spot!

Reservations

Points of Interest

Yellowstone features dramatic canyons, alpine rivers, lush forests, hot springs and gushing geysers, including its most famous, Old Faithful. It's also home to hundreds of animal species, including bears, wolves, bison, elk and antelope.

Grand Canyon National Park

Location

Located in Arizona, Grand Canyon National Park encompasses 277 miles (446 km) of the Colorado River and adjacent uplands.

Waterfalls

The spectacular waterfalls and isolated community within the Havasupai Indian Reservation attract thousands of visitors each year. The Havasupai are intimately connected to the water and the land. When you enter their land, be respectful, you are entering their home.

Where to stay

Lodging and camping! Make sure to reserve your spot!

Reservations

Points of Interest

There are an estimated 1,000 caves within the canyon, but only 335 have been recorded. Only one cave is open to the public.
